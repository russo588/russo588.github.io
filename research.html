Lifting Commuting 3-Isometric Tuples

An operator $T$ is called a $3-$isometry if there exist operators $B_1(T^*,T)$ and $B_2(T^*,T)$ such that $$Q(n) = T^{*n}T^{n} = 1 + nB_1(T^*,T)+n^2B_2(T^*,T)$$ for all natural numbers $n$. An operator $J$ is a Jordan operator of order $2$ if $J=U+N$ where $U$ is unitary, $N$ is nilpotent order $2$, and $U$ and $NN$ commute. An easy computation shows that $J$ is a $3-$isometry and that the restrictions of $J$ to an invariant subspace is also a $3-$isometry. Those $3-$isometries which are the restriction of a Jorrdan operator to an invariant subspace can be identified, uusing the theory of completely positive maps, in terms of a positivity condition on the operator pencil $Q(x)$. In this article, we establish the analogous result in the multi-variable setting and show, by modifying an example of Choi, that an additional hypothesis is necessary. Lastly we discuss the joint spectrum of suub-Jordan tuples and derive the results ofr $3-$symmetric operators as a corrolary.

A lifting theorem for $3-$isometries

An operator $T$ on Hilbert space is a $3-$isometry if there exists operators $B_1$ and $D_2$ such that $$T^{*n}T^n = I+nB_1 +n^2 B_2.$$ An operator $J$ is a Jordan operator if it the sum of a unitary $U$ and nilpotent $N$ of order two which commute. If $T$ is a $3-$isometry and $c>0$, then $I-c^{-2} B_2 + sB_1 + s^2B_2$ is positive semidefinite for all real $s$ if and only if it is the restriction of a Jordan operator $J = U + N$ with the norm of $N$ at most $c$. As a corollary, an analogous result for $3-$symmetric operators, due to Helton and Agler, is recovered.
